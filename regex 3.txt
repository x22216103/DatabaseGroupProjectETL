^-match  the expression to its right at the start of a string before \n
ie  coffee= \^cof

$- matches the expression tothe left at the end of a string 
ie coffee = \$fee

.- Matches expressionto its left except line terminators like \n

car regex

\w matches alphanumeric characters and underscore

\d matches digits
\s matches whitespace
\S matches nonwhitspace
\b matches word boundarys like the startt and end of a word
\B matches the boundary of \w characters
\A  the expression to its right at the absolute start of a string in both single and multiline mode
\Zthe expression to its left at the absolute start of a string in both single and multiline mode
